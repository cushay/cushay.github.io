Welcome! My name is Chris and in this space I am in the process of building a portfolio of my professional experiences, interests, achievements, and goals. As of early 2024, I have graduated with a PhD from Princeton University's Department of Chemical & Biological Engineering, where I researched mechanical relationships between complex flows and deformable structures in the lab of Pierre-Thomas Brun. Some of my published doctoral research has aimed to answer questions such as: how can we leverage capillary action to aid in the fabrication of advanced soft materials? How do confined drainage flows respond to the presence of an elastic medium? More broadly, I aim to unite an interest in solving complex problems with a focus towards communication, thoughtful design, and community-building to build a more sustainable and connected future.
