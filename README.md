My name is Christopher Ushay. As of Fall 2023, I have completed my doctorate at Princeton University under adviser Pierre-Thomas Brun. In my research, I studied the complex mechanics between confined flows, deformable media, and rigid obstacles. In the Liquids & Elasticity Laboratory, we leverage a broad number of tools to aid in our research, both in silico, as in numerical simulations and data visualization, and in situ on the laboratory bench scale, which includes advanced fabrication, photography, and careful experimental design. In using this wide array of techniques over the course of my PhD, I have grown to care deeply about holistic frameworks of science, education, and design, as well as accessibility and transfer of information in our society.

Previously, I graduated from the Macaulay Honors College at the City University of New York with a bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering, where I was the Grove School of Engineering's valedictorian for the Class of 2017.
