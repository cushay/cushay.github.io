I am a scientist with a research background in the mechanics of fluids and deformable materials. As of Fall 2023, I have completed my doctorate at Princeton University under adviser Pierre-Thomas Brun. In my research, I studied the complex mechanics between confined flows, deformable media, and rigid obstacles. In the Liquids & Elasticity Laboratory, we leverage a broad number of tools to aid in our research, both *in silico*, as in numerical simulations and data visualization, and *in situ* on the laboratory bench scale, including advanced fabrication, photography, and thoughtful experimental design. With exposure to such a wide array of techniques over the course of my PhD, I have grown to care deeply about holistic frameworks of science, education, and design, as well as accessibility and transfer of information in our society.

Previously, I graduated from the Macaulay Honors College at the City University of New York with a bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering, where I was the Grove School of Engineering's valedictorian for the Class of 2017. Before starting graduate school, I spent a year working at a Manhattan-based startup concerned with developing machine learning-driven software platforms for biometric identification for developing economies without strong identity infrastructure.
